SpinLaunch Structural & Kinetic-Assist Analysis

Two complementary studies analyzing the structural dynamics of centrifugal launch systems and the orbital benefits of kinetic pre-acceleration.

Why SpinLaunch?

Chemical rockets spend most of their mass fighting gravity. SpinLaunch shifts part of the energy burden to ground infrastructure, trading propellant for mechanical complexity.

The challenge is no longer propulsion but surviving the structural and dynamic loads created by extreme rotational energy.

The Structure Problem

Conventional SpinLaunch releases a single payload and immediately drops their counterweight. This avoids sustained imbalance but creates:

  • Large transient hinge forces
  • Severe post-release oscillations
  • Complete loss of stored rotational energy

Sequential Dual-Payload Concept

This project proposes an alternative: two payloads launched sequentially, separated by 180°.

The second payload acts as the counterweight for the first, canceling angular momentum and reusing stored kinetic energy.

Highly idealized, physics-consistent model

Sequential Release Timing

A fixed half-revolution delay is insufficient for sequential release. The first payload detachment introduces an impulsive disturbance that immediately alters system dynamics. Angular velocity drops, hinge compliance excites wobble, and vacuum seal rupture introduces nonlinear energy loss. Time-based prediction becomes invalid within milliseconds.

STATE-AWARE SOLUTION

\[ \Delta t_{opt} \approx \frac{\Delta \theta_{remaining}}{\omega_{current}} \]

Release timing is recomputed continuously using measured angular state, ensuring correct 180° phase alignment despite transient disturbances.

Limitations

Results represent idealized upper bounds. Models neglect structural flexure, aero-heating, sensor latency, and real-world loss mechanisms.

MuJoCo results are structurally indicative, not experimentally validated.
