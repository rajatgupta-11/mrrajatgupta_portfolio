Kinematics & Trajectory

The core challenge of the 18-DOF system is resolving the $(x, y, z)$ position of each leg tip into Coxa, Femur, and Tibia joint angles.

Triangular Geometry Approach

Instead of black-box libraries, I derived a trigonometric IK solver. This approach ensures a fundamental understanding of coordinate frame transformations and allows for specific mechanical offsets to be hard-coded into the gait cycle.

Bézier Path Planning

Standard linear movement causes massive instantaneous acceleration (jerk), leading to servo torque spikes and system brownouts. I implemented cubic Bézier curves to smooth the foot trajectories.

$$B(t) = (1-t)^3P_0 + 3(1-t)^2tP_1 + 3(1-t)t^2P_2 + t^3P_3$$

Mechanical Design

Designing for 18 micro-servos (MG90S) required a rigorous focus on weight-to-torque ratios and joint reliability.

Pre-Bent Neutral Stance

I engineered the leg geometry with a pre-set neutral bend. This minimizes the holding torque required for the "standing" state, preventing servo stall and thermal runaway during static phases.

The Horn Housing Crisis

Initial models faced a critical failure: the servo horns would slip or detach under high load. I redesigned the joints with a specialized internal indent and a "locking lip" to secure the horn within the 3D-printed assembly.

Fabrication & Craftsmanship

Printed on a Bambu Lab X1E in high-rigidity PLA, the fabrication focused on monolithic structural integrity.

Friction Stir Welding (The Dremel Method)

Standard adhesives proved insufficient for the horn-to-frame bond. I developed a post-processing technique using a rotary tool and raw PLA filament to friction-weld the locking lip over the servo horn.

This created a fused mechanical connection that is far stronger than epoxy and lighter than using additional M3 fasteners, keeping the total assembly weight to a targeted 350g.

Electronics & Power Isolation

Coordinating 18 servos creates "dirty" power rails with massive current transients.

Tethered R&D Phase

To isolate gait-tuning from power distribution issues, V1 is tethered to a high-amperage bench supply. This provides the stable $I_{peak}$ required for testing trajectory smoothing without the brownout risks posed by standard buck converters.

System Integration

Two PCA9685 boards manage the PWM signals, while a state machine in the Arduino controller limits the gait pattern to no more than 5 active motors per phase, protecting the system from current-induced resets.
