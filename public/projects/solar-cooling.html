Closed-Loop Evaporative Cooling for Photovoltaic Panels

Active temperature regulation using feedback control and physical validation.

Thermal Efficiency Loss

I built a closed-loop evaporative cooling system that treats PV temperature as a controllable state.

Efficiency Loss ~0.45% per 1°C > 25°C
Power Loss 12–22% thermal loading

Results at a Glance

Temp Reduction 5°C burst cooling
Energy Recovered ~100 J lab scale
Sim to Hardware Fidelity High Simulink & ARDUINO

Hardware Integration

  • PV panel with surface-mounted NTC thermistor
  • Ultrasonic misting module as cooling actuator
  • Arduino-based controller with relay logic
  • External supply and transistor isolation
  • Independent heat and illumination sources

Simulation & Validation

MATLAB / Simulink was used to predict system behavior before hardware testing.

  • Simulated panel temperature vs measured temperature
  • Cooling cycle timing vs misting actuation
  • Thermal response shape and settling behavior

Experimental results closely matched simulation, validating the thermal model and control assumptions used during design.

Key Limitations & Tradeoffs

  • Actuator energy dominates at small scale
  • Binary control limits resolution
  • Indoor irradiance limits extrapolation
  • Non-uniform misting causes spatial gradients
  • Power measurement uncertainty
