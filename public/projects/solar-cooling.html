PV Thermal Regulation: Closed-Loop Evaporative Cooling

A control-oriented approach to mitigating temperature-induced PV efficiency loss through active evaporative cooling.

System-Level Performance Losses

Photovoltaic systems experience significant performance degradation due to thermal and environmental factors during normal operation.

Efficiency Loss ~0.45% Per 1°C > 25°C
Power Loss 12–22% Thermal Baseline

Results at a Glance

Temp Reduction 5–7 °C
System State Stable
Cycle Match Repeatable

A closed-loop evaporative cooling system was implemented and tested under controlled thermal loading. The controller produced stable actuation cycles that closely matched Simulink predictions.

System Overview

The system consists of a photovoltaic panel instrumented with a thermistor, an ultrasonic misting actuator, and a microcontroller implementing closed-loop temperature regulation.

Thermal and electrical effects were intentionally decoupled using separate illumination and heating sources, allowing temperature dynamics to be isolated and controlled.

Control-Oriented Modeling

The system is modeled as first-order dynamical components derived from energy balance and thermal transport principles.

The objective is a minimal representation that preserves dominant time constants, gains, and delays relevant to closed-loop stability.

Controller Design & Stability

The controller was designed to explicitly match the slow thermal dynamics of the panel, prioritizing stability over aggressive regulation.

A second-order controller with real, negative poles ensures non-oscillatory behavior, while temperature-based relay logic implements practical on–off cooling.

Hardware Integration

Implemented on an Arduino-based platform interfacing with an NTC thermistor and an ultrasonic misting module.

Control Architecture & Supervisory Logic

Panel temperature is sampled at fixed intervals and compared against thresholds. Cooling is activated above the upper threshold, deactivated below the lower threshold, and held constant between them to prevent rapid actuator cycling.

Experimental Results

Closed-loop evaporative cooling reduced peak panel temperature and mitigated temperature-induced power degradation under sustained thermal loading.

While absolute gains are limited by indoor irradiance, observed trends match expected temperature–efficiency relationships.

Energy Balance

Consumed (Cycle) 2000 J
Recovered (Est.) ~100 J

This negative energy balance is expected at laboratory scale and validates the framework rather than net energy performance.

Key Limitations & Tradeoffs
  • Scale mismatch between actuator overhead and recovered power
  • Indoor irradiance limits absolute power extrapolation
  • Binary actuation restricts control resolution
  • Non-uniform mist distribution causes spatial gradients
  • Measurement uncertainty from sensor placement and power sensing
  • Short-duration testing limits long-term assessment
