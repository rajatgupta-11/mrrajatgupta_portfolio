Solutions Looking for Problems

A self-initiated product design series focused on reducing everyday user friction through fast, constraint-driven iteration.

Design Philosophy

The work prioritizes practical impact over novelty, treating small, widely tolerated problems as valid engineering targets when they can be addressed within a couple of days and a $20–30 budget.

Each artifact is intentionally over-engineered relative to its apparent simplicity, emphasizing repetition and volume: making concrete mechanical decisions quickly, validating them in hardware, and moving on once the solution is good enough to disappear into daily use.

Design Constraints

Origin: MTA Desk Clock

The MTA Desk Clock directly inspired this series. Beyond revisiting a side of mechanical engineering I enjoy, it highlighted how much friction can be removed from everyday life through simple, well-considered physical systems.

The project simultaneously improved my instincts as a builder, reinforcing the value of fast iteration and hardware validation over extended design refinement.

Artifact #1

Cantilevered Paper Roll Holder

A wall-mounted, elevated roll holder designed to support the full mass of a standard paper roll while remaining lightweight, tool-less to assemble, and fast to manufacture. Clearances were tuned to allow repeatable assembly without post-processing or fasteners. The cantilevered configuration eliminates the need for through-shaft support, reducing installation complexity.

a

Design Decisions

Geometry, wall thickness, and print orientation were selected to balance stiffness, print time, and FDM tolerances, with layer direction aligned to the primary bending loads.

Clearances were tuned to allow repeatable assembly without post-processing or fasteners. The cantilevered configuration eliminates the need for through-shaft support, reducing installation complexity.

Manufacturing Strategy

Layer direction is intentionally aligned with the primary bending loads to maximize structural performance within FDM constraints.

Wall thickness and infill were calibrated to achieve sufficient rigidity without excessive print time, treating material distribution as a tradeoff between strength and manufacturing efficiency.

Tip: Print both parts twice and invert the bodies during mounting for symmetric load distribution and visual balance.
