Overview

An experimental study analyzing how build orientation affects mechanical behavior and defect formation in DMLS-printed 316L stainless steel.

Vertically printed specimens were manufactured and evaluated through tensile, impact, and hardness testing to characterize anisotropy, failure behavior, and print-induced defects.

Equipment & Material

  • Printer XACT Metal XM200G2
  • Process Direct Metal Laser Sintering (DMLS)
  • Material TruForm 316L Stainless Steel
  • Powder Size ~15–45 μm
  • Powder Mix 20% virgin / 80% recycled
  • Build Orientation 90° (vertical)

Mechanical Testing

Specimens were subjected to a comprehensive suite of standardized mechanical tests to evaluate material performance and identify failure modes.

  • Tensile Testing To Failure
  • Impact Testing Charpy Method
  • Hardness Testing Vickers
  • Inspection Dimensional & Visual Defect

Failure & Defect Observation

  • Fracture behavior consistent with inter-layer weakness
  • Visual evidence of porosity, lack-of-fusion regions, and micro-cracks
  • One specimen failed prematurely due to a print-induced micro-crack
  • Hardness varied across specimens, indicating non-uniform heat input during fabrication

Defect distribution and severity were directly correlated with build orientation, highlighting the critical role of layer-wise construction in final part integrity.
